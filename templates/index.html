Information

Blood pressure (BP) measures the force of blood pushing against artery walls. Maintaining healthy BP levels is crucial for overall cardiovascular health and preventing diseases like heart attack and stroke.

Stages

  • Stage 1: Normal: Systolic ≦ 120 mm Hg, Diastolic ≦ 80 mm Hg
  • Stage 2: Elevated: Systolic 121-139 mm Hg, Diastolic ≦ 80 mm Hg
  • Stage 3: Hypertension: Systolic 140-159 mm Hg, Diastolic ≦ 90 mm Hg
  • Stage 4: Hypertension: Systolic ≦ 160 mm Hg, Diastolic ≦ 90 mm Hg

Risk Factors

Family history increases risk. High salt, low potassium diets. Lack of physical activity. Excessive alcohol consumption. Increases strain on heart and blood vessels long-term stress raises BP risk with age.
